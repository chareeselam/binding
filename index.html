Colophon

This collection gathers writing on the instability of color and how it slips between perception, culture, and technology. Drawing from philosophy, pedagogy, personal memory, and contemporary media, the texts examine color as both a material phenomenon and a psychological construction. Together, they trace the ways context alters what we see, how our minds negotiate those shifts, and how modern visual culture amplifies them. These pieces form a study of color not as a fixed fact but as a continual negotiation between light, surface, and the observer.
